01 · The chapter's question

Winning an election through organizing
is one achievement.

Governing through it is another.

02 · Lit review · 1 of 3

Co-governance has four lineages.

1 · Indigenous–state

Aotearoa, Canada. Formal Crown–Iwi authority over natural resources.

2 · Collaborative governance

Ansell & Gash 2008. Agencies + stakeholders, consensus-oriented, technocratic.

3 · Participatory democracy

Ostrom 1990; Porto Alegre PB (suspended 2017 after PT lost locally).

4 · Movement–elected partnership

Engler & Engler 2022. Durable relationships between grassroots orgs and movement candidates in office.

U.S. organizers borrow vocabulary from all four. They mostly mean the fourth — and it's the most undertheorized.

02 · Lit review · 2 of 3 · Tarlau

Contentious co-governance: the closest fit.

Movements are most effective when they combine disruption with institutional pressure — simultaneously, not sequentially.

Contention only produces concessions where the base behind it is strong enough to make it costly to ignore.

The MST stress test: 40 years, ~2M members, allied Lula government — and settlements collapsed from ~45,000 families/year to under 4,000. Open letter, July 2025: "Lula, where is the Agrarian Reform?"

Tarlau identifies how a base engages the state. She does not theorize what makes a base strong enough to engage at all.

02 · Lit review · 3 of 3 · Ostrom

Ostrom describes the prior condition.

The eight design principles aren't about engaging the state. They describe the internal constitution a group must have to act as a co-governing partner.

  1. Clear boundaries
  2. Rules matched to local conditions
  3. Collective-choice arenas
  4. Monitoring
  5. Graduated sanctions
  6. Low-cost conflict resolution
  7. Recognized right to organize
  8. Nested enterprises

The translation is analogical, but the analogy holds: an organized base is a commons — trust, attention, capacity — vulnerable to free-riding and requiring governance rules to sustain.

03 · The thesis

What co-governance actually requires.

Co-governance = Ostrom (internal) + Tarlau (external)

People-first politics travels where Ostrom conditions are met in the meso-level base.

Where they aren't, you get the appearance of co-governance without the substance.

05 · The Hochul rupture · Patrick × Grace

The rupture that built the structure.

January 2026. Mamdani endorses Hochul as part of a childcare deal. DSA finds out 24–48 hours before. What follows is, on its face, a near-censure — and produces a permanent inside-outside meeting structure that did not exist before.

05 · Hochul · how it landed
When Zohran endorsed Hochul, it was really annoying. They gave us a couple — maybe a day or two — a heads up, but not enough for us to change any strategy or shift a narrative. Grace, NYC-DSA co-chair
The target of the campaign had always been Hochul, and then Zohran tells us he's endorsing Hochul. This was in the midst of the NYSNA strike, where she had been issuing executive orders enabling hospitals to hire scabs. Grace
05 · Hochul · the statement
Steering committee had an emergency meeting. We wrote a statement that was basically almost a censure of Zohran. As a courtesy, we sent that to the administration before we put it out, and they called us and asked us to edit it. Grace
One thing that demonstrates Zohran and his core team's commitment to DSA is that they weren't just like, "Well, fuck you guys." They took that very seriously. Grace
05 · Hochul · the structure that followed
That's when we started having regular meetings, not only with senior administrative officials, which we do every week now, but also with Zohran himself. We meet every other week. Grace
Elle was like, "Okay, something is not working. The kind of inside-outside communication is not effective. We need to fix this." Grace
05 · Hochul · the deficit, named

Someone in the administration implied there was no world in which DSA would have agreed Zohran should endorse Hochul. Grace's response:

"There may not have been. But you also didn't explain your thinking to us. We never knew the trade-offs until you told us what the decision was." Grace, to Elle
05 · Hochul · the punchline
"The trust is there, but the trust is not enough without structure.

And we need creativity to figure out what the structure should be." Grace, NYC-DSA co-chair

10 · Closing

What we cannot yet resolve.

Three open questions for the draft:

  • Can the volunteer base sustain mobilization through the budget cycle without being absorbed into Organize NYC?
  • Does the new inside-outside meeting structure survive its first substantive disagreement — not procedural like Hochul, but on a budget priority?
  • Are Cea's institutional handles — community boards, Rent Board, tenant associations — being populated, or merely staffed?
